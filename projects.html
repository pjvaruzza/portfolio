Utility · Grid

Southern California Edison

Distribution Engineer · DER Integration · 2022 – Present

Background

Southern California Edison (SCE) is one of the largest investor-owned utilities in the US, supplying electricity to 15 million people across a 50,000 sq mi service territory. As a Distribution Engineer, my role focuses on accelerating the integration of Distributed Energy Resources (DERs) onto the grid, aligned with the California Public Utility Commission (CPUC) High DER Future Grid Proceeding. I have built a deep understanding of how the utility industry is responding to unprecedented load growth and grid decarbonization challenges.

Key Contributions

  • Distribution grid planning and engineering for DER interconnection at scale
  • Non-wires alternatives cost-benefit analysis to defer traditional infrastructure upgrades
  • Data analysis and process automation to improve planning workflows
  • CPUC regulatory compliance and High DER Future Grid proceeding support
  • Supporting SCE's "Countdown to 2045" climate targets and enabling statewide electrification
  • Cross-functional collaboration with rates, policy, and operations teams

Skills Applied

  • Distribution grid planning & engineering
  • Non-wires alternatives & cost-benefit analysis
  • Project management & data automation
  • CPUC regulatory compliance
Startup · Founder

Hubbub

Founder · Circular Economy Platform · 2020 – 2022

Background

Hubbub.shop was a circular economy startup focused on elimating the carbon emissions generated by linear consumer supply chains by making it cheaper, more convenient, and more enjoyable for consumers to rent everyday items instead of buying new. I launched it out of Columbia University in 2020, frustrated by seeing the environmental destruction and sheer waste of materials and energy stemming from the over-extraction of resources to produce all the stuff in our lives that inevitably is destined for landfill. caused by the massive volumes of consumer waste sent to landfill each year. In January 2020, I recruited rock star co-founders who shared the convicition that to move the needle, we needed to build the technical and logistics infrastructure that would make circular supply chains desirable, cost effective, and delightful for consumers. Together, we took the concept from 0 to 1, building our own technology platform, logistics management system, and growing the company to servce thousands of users across New York City.

Key Results

  • Raised pre-seed capital from a startup incubator through the full venture fundraising process — pitching angels, climate tech investors, and institutional programs — deploying funds to scale NYC operations
  • Grew to 400+ active users per month with 288% year-over-year revenue growth
  • Kept 210 tonnes of items out of landfill, embodying an estimated 1,320 tonnes of CO₂e avoided
  • Achieved positive unit economics through a micro-fulfillment hub model that minimized last-mile delivery costs and made the business operationally viable at early scale
  • Built proprietary data analytics and logistics tooling to forecast demand patterns and automate rental order fulfillment, reducing operational overhead
  • Conducted 300+ customer discovery interviews to pressure-test assumptions and drive rapid product iteration — running weekly sprint cycles informed by direct user feedback
  • Assembled and led a cross-functional founding team spanning engineering, operations, and business development, expanding service coverage across New York City
clean energy · infrastructure

Stealth Project

Founder · Clean Energy Infrastructure · 0000 – 0000

Background

A stealth-stage startup developing a novel logistics infrastructure for the clean hydrogen economy. The core thesis: the locations best suited for low-cost green hydrogen production — remote areas with abundant wind, solar, and hydroelectric resources — are rarely co-located with the industrial and urban demand centers that need it most. Existing transport solutions (pipelines, compressed tube trailers, liquefied hydrogen tankers) are either capital-prohibitive, lossy, or decades away from the scale required. We're building a different answer. The venture is commercializing hydrogen-carrying airships as a flexible, low-infrastructure modality for moving green hydrogen and energy from generation sites to demand centers — unlocking stranded renewable resources and accelerating the economics of industrial decarbonization.

Current Focus

  • Defining the technical architecture and go-to-market for an airship-based hydrogen logistics network
  • Mapping the highest-value origin-destination corridors where stranded renewables meet hard-to-abate industrial demand
  • Building the founding team and advancing early conversations with potential strategic partners and investors
